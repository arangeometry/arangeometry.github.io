About

Arannya Bhattacherjee, driven by a passion for mathematics, is an emerging researcher exploring the intersections of algebraic geometry and algebraic topology, with a primary focus on mirror symmetry and its transformative role in modern mathematical theory. He is a Master’s Graduate in Mathematics, interested in Algebraic Geometry and Algebraic Topology, and currently studying Moduli Spaces of projective curves. Also, he is interested in different Homological and Cohomological aspects that arise as interplay between Geometry and Topology.


He is also interested in Birational geometry, a branch of algebraic geometry that studies algebraic varieties up to birational equivalence, where two varieties are equivalent if they are isomorphic outside a lower-dimensional subset. Its goal is to classify and analyze varieties through transformations that preserve their rational structure.


Central themes include the Minimal Model Program (MMP), which seeks simpler canonical forms for varieties, such as minimal models or Mori fiber spaces, and the classification of varieties into types like Fano, Calabi-Yau, or varieties of general type based on properties of the canonical divisor Kx. Birational geometry also addresses rationality questions (e.g., whether a variety is birationally equivalent to projective space) and the resolution of singularities, preserving birational equivalence while simplifying the structure. By connecting algebraic methods with geometric insights, birational geometry reveals the intrinsic nature of varieties and their relationships.

Mirror symmetry is a concept he works on that explores a duality between pairs of Calabi-Yau manifolds. It connects their geometric and symplectic properties, blending ideas from algebraic geometry and physics. He focuses on its implications in moduli spaces, enumerative geometry, and how it bridges theoretical math with practical physical applications.
