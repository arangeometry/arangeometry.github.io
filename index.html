About

Arannya Bhattacherjee, driven by a passion for mathematics, is an emerging researcher exploring the intersections of algebraic geometry and algebraic topology, with a primary focus on mirror symmetry and its transformative role in modern mathematical theory.

I am a Master’s Graduate in Mathematics, interested in Algebraic Geometry and Algebraic Topology, and currently studying Moduli Spaces of projective curves. Also, I am interested in different Homological and Cohomological aspects that arise as interplay between Geometry and Topology.

Mirror symmetry is a concept I work on that explores a duality between pairs of Calabi-Yau manifolds. It connects their geometric and symplectic properties, blending ideas from algebraic geometry and physics. I study its implications in moduli spaces, enumerative geometry, and how it bridges theoretical math with practical physical applications.
